“More speed, more power with minimum time”


Bots up is a robotics event in which the terrain conquering skills and brute force of the bot is put to a test. The bots will be put in a challenging and fun arena. Design a manually controlled ROBOT that has capacity to cover maximum distance in shortest possible time, challenging the hurdles and to be one of the best opponents. On its way to the finish line, the robot has to traverse sharp turns and many hurdles like a ramp that could set it flying off the track, gravel, sand, ramps etc.. It’s a race against time !! So, come and enjoy the exciting Roborace with your friends and win exciting prizes.

Team specification


Maximum 4 members per team allowed.
Student from different institutes can from a team.

Machine Specification


The machine should fit within a box of dimensions (300x300x300) mm at the start of the game.
The Machine should not weigh more than 3 KG.
Both wireless and wired machines are allowed.
Use of readymade toy is not allowed, but one can use the readymade circuits.
Chemicals, compressed gases and rockets, fuels, combustion power methods, etc. are strictly prohibited. However commercial batteries like dry cells and lead acid are allowed to use.

Rules and regulations


Power Supply
Participants will be allowed to use maximum onboard 12V DC power. Use of heavy equipment like compressors is banned.

Safety
The machines you build will be first checked for size and safety. The machine should not cause electrical power breakdowns. The robots must not contain any combustible, corrosive, or otherwise dangerous materials. No explosive, compressive or expansive, either internal or external is permitted. Any robot whose strategy or operation is considered dangerous can be disqualified by the judges at their discretion.


Game Field


The width of the track is 300 mm.
The entire game field is divided into no. of sub zones. Each zone contains various hurdles which are as follows:
• Bridges
• bumpers
• Sand
• There can be another 2 to 3 surprise hurdles on the field.
Participants have to start the game from starting zone and reach to finish line in minimum possible time.


Point System


• The game field is divided in no. of zones and each zone has some points.
The team whose robot reaches the finish line in minimum time is declared as the winner
• If the participant is not able to complete the game field than points will be given on the basis of no. of sub zones completed.

Foul/Penalties


• The robot is not allowed to go out of the track or sub zone. If it goes out of track or sub zone than violation will be considered and accordingly points will be deducted as per judges discretion. After that, the participants have to start the game from the starting of that particular zone. While doing so the participants will be allowed to put the robot by hands.
• The robots are not allowed to leave part(s) on any part of the arena. In case some part of the machine falls on the arena, the penalty (including disqualification) will be based on judges’ discretion.
• Machines damaging any part of the arena will be disqualified. Damaging does not include unintentional scratches on the any surface by the machine. Once again judge’s discretion will be used.
• During game the participants are not allowed to go inside the game field.
• Decision taken by the judges would be final and would not be challenged
